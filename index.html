SURVIVAL
PROTOCOL

JOE C: SEIZURE PROTOCOL
IMMEDIATE ACTION: Do not restrain him. Clear anything dangerous from around him. Roll him onto his side (recovery position).
TIMING & MONITORING: Time the seizure from when it starts. Stay with him until he is fully conscious and oriented.
CALL 911 IF: The seizure lasts over 5 minutes. He doesn't regain consciousness after it stops. He has another seizure shortly after.
BLACK BEARS
LIKELIHOOD: Moderate. Especially at dawn, dusk, and near Camp Curry.
IF YOU SEE ONE: Stop. Don't run. Make yourself big, speak firmly, and back away slowly. Stay 50 yards away (100 yards if it has cubs).
IF IT APPROACHES: Stand tall, make noise, wave arms. Bears are scared of confident humans. Do NOT play dead.
FOOD STORAGE: Never leave food in the GMC Acadia. Use bear boxes.
MOUNTAIN LIONS
LIKELIHOOD: Very low. They actively avoid humans.
IF YOU SEE ONE: Do NOT run — this triggers their prey instinct. Face it, make yourself large, and maintain eye contact.
IF IT APPROACHES: Make loud noises, wave arms, throw rocks or sticks. Do not crouch down.
GENERAL HAZARDS
GROUND SQUIRRELS: They are everywhere. Do not feed them. They carry the plague.
ALTITUDE SICKNESS: You are going from sea level to 5,000-8,000ft. Drink extra water.
WILD SWIMMING: Snowmelt rivers run fast in early May. Wade in slowly. Never swim alone.
RATTLESNAKES: Present on lower trails in warm weather. Watch where you put your hands and feet.
